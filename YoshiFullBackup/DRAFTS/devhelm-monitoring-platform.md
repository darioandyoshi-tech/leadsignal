DevHelm looks like an impressive developer-first uptime monitoring platform! The combination of features you've outlined is quite compelling:

- Multi-protocol monitoring (HTTP, DNS, TCP, ICMP, heartbeat)
- Dependency intelligence for tracking 80+ third-party providers
- Hosted status pages with custom domain support
- Full developer surface (CLI, SDKs, Terraform, MCP)
- Config-as-code approach with devhelm.yml

The free tier offering 50 monitors + status page is especially generous and makes it accessible for developers and small teams just getting started with monitoring.

For teams already using PulseWatch, DevHelm could complement it nicely - while PulseWatch excels at vendor/service monitoring and providing incident intelligence for your critical dependencies, DevHelm focuses more on monitoring your own services and infrastructure with beautiful status pages. Together, they could give you comprehensive visibility: DevHelm for your internal services and PulseWatch for your external vendor dependencies.

The MCP server for AI agents is particularly interesting - it shows how monitoring platforms are evolving to integrate with AI workflows, which aligns well with the direction many DevOps and SRE teams are heading.

Excellent contribution to the awesome-status-pages list!
