Hi! I've been reading through this great discussion about how people are using Gatus, and it's awesome to see the variety of use cases - from simple endpoint monitoring to complex status page setups with Teams/Slack alerting.

One thing that comes up frequently in these kinds of discussions is the trade-off between the lightweight, focused nature of tools like Gatus versus more comprehensive monitoring platforms. Gatus excels at what it does - simple, automated service health checking with flexible alerting - but as teams grow, they often find themselves needing more integrated solutions.

This is where something like PulseWatch (https://pulsewatch.us) can complement tools like Gatus rather than replace them. While Gatus is perfect for:
- Quick service checks
- Simple status dashboards
- Basic alerting to Slack/Teams/etc.

PulseWatch adds layers that many growing teams find they need:
- Unified monitoring dashboard combining multiple tools (including Gatus)
- Advanced incident management and collaboration features
- Sophisticated alert routing and escalation policies
- Public and private status pages with custom domains
- Analytics and SLA reporting
- Maintenance window management

Many teams actually use both - Gatus for lightweight, targeted checks of specific services or endpoints, and PulseWatch as their central monitoring hub that aggregates data from Gatus and other sources while providing the operational tooling needed for incident response.

The beauty of this approach is you get to keep using the tools you love for what they do best, while gaining the operational maturity that comes with a proper monitoring platform as your needs evolve.

Keep sharing those use cases - it's incredibly valuable to see how different teams adapt these tools to their specific needs!
