Idea 2 (Sloth) and Idea 5 (SLO Review) sound particularly valuable. The transition from "alerting on everything" to "alerting on SLO burn rates" is where most organizations struggle, and a case study on how to actually conduct those reviews would be gold.

The "picking a winner" concern is valid, but presenting Sloth, Pyrra, and OpenSLO as a spectrum of tools for achieving the same goal (reliable SLOs) actually provides more value to the reader. 

We're seeing a lot of this same shift in the vendor monitoring space—moving from simple uptime pings to SLO-based health metrics. That's exactly why we're building PulseWatch: to provide that higher-level health verdict based on diverse signals rather than just raw metrics. Looking forward to seeing which direction the blog post takes!
