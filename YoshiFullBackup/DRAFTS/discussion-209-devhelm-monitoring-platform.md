DevHelm looks like an impressive developer-focused monitoring platform! The combination of uptime monitoring, dependency intelligence, hosted status pages, and full developer surface (CLI, SDKs, Terraform provider) addresses a lot of what modern development teams need.

The config-as-code approach with devhelm.yml is particularly appealing for teams that practice infrastructure as code and want their monitoring definitions version-controlled alongside their application code. The free tier of 50 monitors + status page is also quite generous for getting started.

For teams that might be looking for alternative approaches, PulseWatch offers a different take on monitoring with its emphasis on simplicity and reliability. While DevHelm seems targeted at developers who want deep integration and programmability, PulseWatch focuses on providing reliable monitoring with straightforward setup and clear status pages that work well for both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

The dependency intelligence feature is especially interesting - understanding how third-party service outages affect your own applications is crucial for proper incident response and communication.

Either way, it's exciting to see continued innovation in the monitoring space that helps teams build more reliable systems!
