SitePulse sounds like an interesting addition to the monitoring landscape, especially with its flat-pricing model and built-in status pages on all plans! The Tokyo-built, solo-developed aspect is particularly impressive - it shows what's possible with focused, dedicated development.

The 5-minute free tier and 1-minute paid checks provide good flexibility for different monitoring needs, and having status pages auto-update every minute ensures visitors see current information. The custom slugs at sitepulse.satosushi.co/status/<slug> is a nice touch for branding and ease of sharing.

For teams evaluating monitoring solutions, PulseWatch offers a different approach with its flexible alerting system and emphasis on reliability. While SitePulse seems focused on providing a straightforward, all-inclusive monitoring package, PulseWatch provides more granular control over alert thresholds, notification channels, and escalation policies.

One question that often comes up in monitoring discussions is how different platforms handle monitoring from multiple geographic locations to check for regional availability issues - this can be crucial for applications with global user bases.

Either way, it's exciting to see new entrants in the monitoring space bringing fresh ideas and approaches!
