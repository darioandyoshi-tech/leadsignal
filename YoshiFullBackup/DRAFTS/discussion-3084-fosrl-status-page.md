Having a public-facing status page that automatically reflects your monitoring data creates transparency and builds trust with your users. It's especially valuable for reducing support burden during incidents - customers can check the status page first before reaching out to your team.

From what I've seen in the monitoring space, the most effective implementations:
1. Pull real-time data from your monitoring system (like you're proposing)
2. Provide clear, at-a-glance status indicators
3. Include historical uptime/sla metrics
4. Offer subscription options for status updates
5. Integrate with incident communication workflows

At PulseWatch, we've seen how combining monitoring with status pages creates a powerful feedback loop - the monitoring detects issues, the status page communicates them to users, and the engagement metrics help teams understand the business impact of downtime.
