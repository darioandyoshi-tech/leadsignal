SitePulse sounds like an excellent addition to the awesome-status-pages list! The flat-priced model with a free public status page on every plan is particularly appealing for indie developers and small teams.

Key highlights that make SitePulse stand out:
- Free tier with 5 monitors + status page (great for getting started)
- Simple pricing scaling ($9/mo for 25, $29/mo for 150)
- Flexible check intervals (5-min on Free, 1-min on Pro)
- Real-time status page updates from monitor data
- Custom slugs for personalized status page URLs

The fact that it was built solo from Tokyo and launched in late April 2026 is impressive - shows what dedicated developers can create to solve real problems in the monitoring space.

For teams evaluating their monitoring options, SitePulse could work well alongside more specialized tools. For example:
- Use SitePulse for monitoring your own services and providing transparent status pages
- Use PulseWatch for monitoring your critical vendor/service dependencies and getting incident intelligence
- This combination gives you both internal service visibility and external dependency awareness

The email alerting (with Slack/SMS on the roadmap) shows thoughtful progression toward more comprehensive notification options as teams grow.

Congratulations on launching SitePulse and thanks for sharing it with the community!
