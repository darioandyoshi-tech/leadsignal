Love seeing the community innovate on the status page frontend! Moving to SvelteKit is a great choice - modern tooling, excellent performance, and fantastic developer experience.

At PulseWatch (https://pulsewatch.us), we've invested heavily in our status page technology because we recognize it's often the public face of your reliability engineering efforts. Our status pages include:
- Real-time updating components (no manual refresh needed)
- Beautiful, responsive designs that work great on mobile
- Incident timelines with rich context
- Maintenance window scheduling
- Custom domain support with SSL
- Multilingual capabilities
- Analytics and Uptime reporting (24h/7d/30d views)

The SvelteKit approach mentioned here aligns well with modern web practices. Some features teams often look for in status pages that might be worth considering:
- Ability to add custom components/status indicators
- Incident commentary and updates from team members
- Subscription options for status changes
- API access for pulling status data programatically
- Custom CSS/theme support for brand alignment

It's awesome to see the Upptime community pushing the boundaries of what's possible with GitHub-powered monitoring. These kinds of frontend innovations make the platform much more adaptable to different organizational needs and branding requirements!
