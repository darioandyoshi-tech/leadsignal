# Autonomous Skill Improvement Experiments
## Experiment Log

This file tracks all autonomous skill improvement experiments conducted using the Adapted Autoresearch Concept.

## Experiment Format

Each experiment should follow this structure:

```
## Experiment #[NUMBER]: [SHORT DESCRIPTION]
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM]
**Hypothesis**: [What specific improvement is being tested]
**Method**: [How the improvement will be implemented]
**Time Budget**: [Fixed duration for the experiment, e.g., "30 minutes"]
**Success Criteria**: [Specific, measurable conditions that must be met]
**Results**: [What actually happened during the experiment]
**Outcome**: [KEPT/DISCARDED] - Based on results vs. success criteria
**Patterns Extracted**: [What was learned for future improvement cycles]
```

## How to Log a New Experiment

To log a new improvement experiment, copy the format above and fill in the details for your specific experiment. Be sure to:

1. **Use sequential numbering** (#2, #3, #4, etc.)
2. **Include precise timestamps**
3. **State hypotheses clearly and testably**
4. **Define specific, measurable success criteria**
5. **Record actual results objectively**
6. **State outcome clearly as KEPT or DISCARDED**
7. **Extract specific patterns for future use**
8. **Keep entries concise but informative**

## Experiment Guidelines

- **Be honest** about results - both successes and failures are valuable for learning
- **Focus on learning** - what matters most is what you extract for future improvement
- **Stay objective** in assessing outcomes against success criteria
- **Look for patterns** across multiple experiments
- **Build on what works** - keep successful approaches and refine them
- **Learn from failures** - understand why something didn't work to avoid similar mistakes
- **Think long-term** - some improvements may show benefits only over multiple cycles
